Comparison of performance of different machine learning methods

Method Accuracy
ZeroR 52.4439 %
kNN(k=1) 66.4465 %
NaiveBayes 76.2219 %
MultilayerPerceptron 76.4861 %
J48 Decision Tree 76.7503 %
NBTree 78.0713 %

We first compare the performance of different Machine Learning methods. Since different machine learning methods have different emphasis and strong points, they may have quite different behavior on the same data set. On the left is the table of accuracy with different methods. The data file contains the price percent change of AMZN, AAPL, FB, MSFT and NASDAQ as features and GOOG's stock condition(increase or decrease) as labels. The result is calculated using Weka. Each method is tested using 10 folds cross validation.

From the result we can see that the base method ZeroR has the worst accuracy, it is nearly the same as the accuracy of choosing labels randomly. K-Nearest-Neighbor behaves better than ZeroR, but still not so good, this is due to the fact that in stock market the condition of nearest points do not necessarily need to be the same. Consider a run chart of a stock we often see in news or finance websites. In a macro point of view, the run chart sorts of revealing the overall performance and achievement of the company in a relatively long time interval. But from the micro point of view, the trend of a stock is more like a random walk rather than something that is inherently correlated. The behavior of each tiny time slice has significant uncertainty due to the random fluctuations of transaction and thus make the run chart of a small portion look like the Brownian motion of particles in a fluid. So in order to deal with this property of the stock we had better use the machine learning methods that introduces probability. In the following methods the accuracy of Naive Bayes classifier, MultilayerPerceptron and J48 decision tree are quite similar. In order to further increase accuracy we can combine decision tree and Naive Bayes classifier to exploit the advantage of both methods. An NBTree where each node of tree is a Naive Bayes classifier is such kind of combination. From the result we can find that NBTree method further increases the accuracy. Although the figure is not very obvious, it can still be considered effective. So in the following experiments we will use NBTree as learning method.

Now explore the influence of duration. On the left is the table of accuracy with different duration. Note that longer duration is not available since many IT companies like Facebook and Twitter started selling stock to the public relatively recently. The data file contains the price percent change of AMZN, AAPL, FB, MSFT and NASDAQ as features and GOOG's stock condition(increase or decrease) as labels. The result is calculated using Weka with NBTree method.

The result might seems counterintuitive at first glance since in the field of machine learning we usually think that the more data, the better. But this may be not the case in the research of stock market. Why? Because stock market is a highly volatile system that evolves rapidly. Unlike the usual machine learning problems with a "static" data set, the data in stock market is dynamic and more recent data often has greater significance than older ones. Thus outdated data will often become the noise of whole data set and decrease the accuracy. On the other hand, too few samples may be less convincing and increase the system randomness. So in the following experiments we will use the data set from 05/29/2013 to 05/29/2016.

The impact of sector of features on accuracy

Now compare the influence of sector on the result's accuracy. Stock market sectors are a way of classifying stocks, wherein stocks in similar industries are grouped together. Studying sector information allows investors to see how a particular stock is performing in relation to its peers. Below is the result of Google's stock prediction with features from different sectors:

Largest IT companies

Accuracy = 78.0713 %

Features include APPL, AMZN, NASDAQ, MSFT and FB's stock price.

Other IT companies

Features include ADBE, EBAY, LNKD, PCLN and YAHOO's stock price.

Hardware Companies

Accuracy = 61.9551 %

Features include CISCO, AMD, INTEL, NVDA and QCOM's stock price.

Traditional industrial companies

Accuracy = 63.0119 %

Features include BOEING, FORD, GE, RDS-A and UNP's stock price.

If we consider accuracy as the correlation of Google's stock performance with these different factors, then from the result we can see that Google's stock price has highest correlation with other large scale IT cooperations like Apple Inc. and Amazon.com, Inc. IT companies which don't have the similar scale as Google in general are less correlated to Google's stock price compared to those biggest IT companies. The most interesting part is that hardware companies have such a low correlation with Google that even the correlation of traditional industrial companies is larger. This may be due to the fact that the hot spot of Computer Technology nowadays lies more in the field of Internet and software development other than hardware progress. So in order to avoid introducing noise, in the following work we will only use stock price of largest IT companies as features.

Future Work

More Label Values

Labels may be subdivided into smaller intervals(e.g, -1%, 0, 1%, etc.) rather than just 0 or 1 to improve the usability of model, but this may decrease accuracy. We will need to conduct further research to see whether this is a practical solution or not. New approximation algorithm may be necessary in order to offset the accuracy loss due to introducing smaller intervals.

Generic model

Instead of predicting a specific stock, we will also try to build a generic model that takes any stock as input and output whether its price will increase or decrease. To achieve this we may need to use unsupervised learning for cluster analysis and grouping data in different sectors.

Economic Statistics

Economics is a field highly related to finance and stock market. Conducting Economics analysis through the introduction of economic statistical data such as consumer price index, currency strength, interest rate, GDP and disposable income and investigate the relationship between these economic data and stock market using machine learning method is a potential entry point to further research.

Profitability Analysis

The ultimate goal of stock market analysis is to find whether an investment is profitable or not. Our current work mainly focuses on the trend analysis of a single stock, to further research on the profitability, unsupervised learning and cluster algorithm for forming portfolios efficiently and accurately from combination of stocks is needed. The ideal solution should be input the stock market condition and output a portfolio with largest profitability.
