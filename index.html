Introduction

Stock market is a very important and interesting system to research on considering its extreme complexity and unpredictability as well as high profitability. Our task is to research how Google's(now Alphabet Inc.) stock price change(in terms of increase (1) or decrease (0)) depends on the stocks that may influence or are highly related to Google's stock.

Why Study This

Profitability

Stock market is a highly profitable area in modern society compared to traditional economic activities like agriculture or manufacturing. Thorough research of stock market is helpful for both personal career and social development.

Shortcoming of human trading

For common human being traders the trading actions are mainly taken on the basis of personal experience, so the trading actions have large uncertainty and are always subjective and thus lack of standardization process.

Shortcoming of human trading

Another flaw of stock market prediction by human is that the decision is always influenced by emotion, so traders sometimes may make some irrational decisions when facing extreme market fluctuation.

complexity and unpredictability

Stock market is a highly complicated system where stock price is influenced by enormous number of factors. Although there are some mathematical finance models such as Black–Scholes model, Binomial options model, etc., they are highly idealized and are mainly used for concept interpretation.

Advantage of machine learning

The above weakness of human trader operation can be reduced or even overcome if we apply machine learning method to help with our prediction. Also we don't need to build a specific model to research stock market and can instead use machine learning to study the behavior of stock. So machine learning is a suitable tool to extract useful information from huge amount of data in stock market.

Approach

It is well known that IT industry is a highly vibrant field nowadays and Google is a typical innovative company in this field, so we decide to predict Google’s stock price. Since stocks in the same sector are often correlated, we can use same sector’s stock price as features to predict the price of another stock in the sector. So we choose Facebook, Amazon, Microsoft, Apple and NASDAQ Composite’s data to get the features. Since it is often the change rate other than absolute price that is more illustrative, the feature is the companies’ stock price change percent. The label is simply 0 and 1 where 0 represents that Google’s stock price is decreasing while 1 represents that Google’s price is increasing.

NBTree algorithm

Through comparing different methods I found that NBTree(Naive Bayes Decision tree) has the best performance. NBTree is a kind of machine learning method that combines the strong points of Naive Bayes Classifier and Decision Tree. In an NBTree the skeleton is still a tree but each leaf node is a Naive Bayes Classifier instead of nodes predicting a single feature.
On the left is the algorithm of generating an NBTree. From the description we can see that the algorithm is in general quite similar to the decision tree algorithm except that when the utility doesn't increase significantly, the algorithm will stop partitioning and end up with a Naive Bayes classifier as the leaf node. This is sort of like a kind of pruning strategy and can thus increase efficiency and avoid over fitting problem. The uncertainty and unpredictability property of stock market also makes NBTree an appropriate method for solving problems like stock with probability.

Data set

The data sets are csv files taken from financial statistics websites like yahoo finance and Quandl. The csv files provide high, low, open and close price in a daily basis, we can use the data to calculate every day’s percent change of price. The training data ranges from May 29,2013 to May 29,2016 on a daily basis and there are about 750 samples. Note that greater number of samples is not available since many IT companies like Facebook and Twitter started selling stock to the public relatively recently. The data set is trained via Weka software package and tested using 10 folds cross validation. Higher folds would be time consuming and lower folds would not be enough to eliminate the problem of over fitting. Consider the extreme complexity of stock market, a result with accuracy up to 70% should be viewed as success.

Key Results

We compared different machine learning methods and found that NBTree has the best performance. We also researched on the size of data set on accuracy and found the interesting fact that accuracy doesn't increase, but rather decrease as the number of example increases. In addition, the impact of sector of features on accuracy is researched. The result shows that Google's stock price is more related to similar large IT companies than hardware companies or other traditional industrial companies.
